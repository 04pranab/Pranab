About

I am an undergraduate researcher with a deep interest in systems programming, computer architecture, and operating system design. My work focuses on understanding how modern processors are designed and how operating systems interact with hardware at the lowest levels.

I approach research with an independent, hands-on mindset—building systems from first principles, experimenting with RISC-V architectures, and exploring the tradeoffs between performance, simplicity, and correctness. I believe in learning through implementation.

My areas of focus include processor microarchitecture, hardware-software co-design, bare-metal programming, and performance analysis. I am committed to rigorous, technically grounded work without oversimplification or hype.
